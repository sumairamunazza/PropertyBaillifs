FAQ's For LanLords

What Do Property Managers Do?

  • They are agents for the landlord (owner).
  • They prepare properties for renting.
  • They inspect properties before, during, and after a tenancy term.
  • They market properties for optimal rental income.
  • They make properties available for prospective tenants to view.
  • They receive applications and then screen them.
  • They coordinate tenancy agreement signings and renewals.
  • They maintain the investment property.
  • They correspond with owners and tenants.
  • They coordinate utility payments during the make ready process.

How much are your Property Management Fees?

Property Bailiffs will get your property ready for showing, interview applicants, collect rents, make repairs, and help with an eviction process. Typically we charge a flat fee per month or a percentage of the monthly gross rent for these services. Percentages could range from 10%-15%.

Other fees that could be charged outside of this monthly commission could be an initial setup fee, tenancy renewal fee, early cancellation of contract, advertising costs, and costs for any repair work.

How will you find tenants for my property?

We utilize multiple avenues in finding qualified tenants to rent your investment property. Property Bailiffs knows and understand the importance of getting quality tenants for your property.

What do you look for in tenants?

- Stability of employment - this will give you an indication of whether the tenant will be likely to stay for the nominated lease period and whether they will have the funds to pay for it.

- Good credit history - ability to pay the rent.

- Avoid over crowding - ask about the number of people who will be living in the property. Over crowding can cause excessive wear and tear.

- Proof of identity - ask the property manager to take a photocopy of a drivers license.

How much will my investment property rent for?

Rental income is determined by several market factors including the condition of the property, location, and the rental rates for similar competing properties in the immediate area.

Unfortunately, the markets that dictate rental rates vary vastly from the markets that influence value of properties. Please contact our office and we can discuss this with you as it relates to your specific property

How do Property Inspections work?

Regular inspections of the property are completed by the property manager when taking on the rental, once every quarterly, and when a tenant moves out, and any other time as requested by the owner.

What is a Tenancy Agreement?

This is a legally binding contract between a landlord and tenant that sets out both the legal and contractual obligations of both parties.

Who is responsible for Utilities?

The tenant is responsible for gas, electricity, telephone, council tax charges and water if not included in a service charge.

How often will I hear from my property manager?

At Property Bailiffs, you have a choice of either:

- Hear from your property manager every time something occurs with the tenancy

- Let your property manager handle situations with your best interests in mind

Advise your property manager of your expectations to avoid confusion. Also discuss your preferred method of communication - be it by email, your home phone, your mobile.

Who transfers the utility supplies?

We at Property Bailiffs will inform the relevant utilities companies and will confirm all transfers in writing however we stress that the authorities will not always take instructions from ourselves.

Does my property be furnished or unfurnished?

There is no specific demand for one or the other, however if it is furnished then you have to ensure that all furniture must comply with current legislation.

All furniture must comply with the 1993 amendments to the Furniture and Furnishings (Fire) (Safety) Regulations 1988 which extends the scope of the Consumer Protection Act 1987 (CPA).

Do I need to reference the applicants?

It is a legal requirement to have the applicant(s) professionally referenced; we employ the service of external referencing company, who are a recognised independent agency and carry out thorough checks including credit report, employer, character & previous landlord reference.

How long is the tenancy agreement for?

Assured Shorthold Tenancy normally starts from 6 months initially; however some tenants may request a longer agreement.

How much deposit is collected?

We request a deposit equivalent to 1 months rent + £100 from all tenants, this is security against damage etc. At the end of the tenancy the deposit is returned to the tenant less charges for any damage.

Do I need insurance for my property?

It is the landlord’s responsibility to insure the building with all contents, fixtures & fittings. You must inform your insurer that you are letting the property; failure to do so may invalidate your policy.

Do I need to inform anyone before renting?

If the property is subject to a mortgage it is your responsibility to seek consent, in writing, from the mortgagee. It is wise to obtain permission in principle prior to marketing your property to avoid unnecessary delays when negotiating a tenancy.

If you are a leaseholder it is important that you obtain approval from your freeholder/management agent to sublet so as not to invalidate your lease. We will be happy to advise them on these matters. These requests are not normally refused.

Do you issue an inventory list?

We issue two copies of the inventory list to the tenant for them to to check and amend where needed. Both copies must signed and dated by the tenant, one returned to us as soon after moving in as possible, one copy kept for their records.

If we do not receive a copy of the inventory from them within 1 week of the tenancy start date, we will assume that it is correct and therefore, the tenants are liable for any errors and irregularities that may be on the inventory when they vacate.

Do you do any property Visits?

The cleanliness of the property and its furnishings are the tenants responsibility and these must be kept in good condition during their stay and carpets etc shampooed if needed, especially before the property is vacated.

We carry out property visits of all the properties we manage on a regular, normally quarterly.

Who is responsible for repairs?

The general maintenance of the property is the responsibility of the landlord except when a repair is necessary due to a tenant’s misuse.

As part of our property management services, if we are managing a property we will arrange for the work to be carried out by one of our approved contractors.

Who is responsible for external maintenance?

External decorations & maintenance are the responsibility of the landlord. However, if the property has a garden the tenant is usually responsible for its upkeep, but the landlord must provide the required necessary tools.

What about safety regulations for rented property?

Issuance of Gas safety certificates annually by a registered engineer is the responsibility of the landlord. In addition you have a duty to ensure that all electrical equipment is safe to use, if necessary Property Bailiff can arrange for NICEIC & PAT tests.

All buildings built since June 1992 must be fitted with mains operated smoke detectors but it advisable to fit detectors in older properties too.

What about pets?

Pets are generally not allowed in any of the properties we manage unless written permission has been granted by the landlord and if they are found to be on the premises without permission the tenants may be asked to re-home them.

What kind of tenancy agreement will be used?

The most common form of tenancy agreement used is an “Assured Shorthold Tenancy” (an AST) under the 1988 Housing Act (amended 1996). This type of tenancy offers the most flexibility to both landlord and tenant; has straightforward notice procedures for bringing the tenancy to an end.

What is a break clause?

A break clause enables the landlord or the tenant to bring the tenancy to an end thus terminating the tenancy earlier than the end of the original fixed term. This clause requires a 2 months written notice after the minimum term.

What does “Jointly and Severally” mean?

Where there is to be more than one adult on the tenancy agreement the contract will state that they are “jointly and severally” responsible. This means that the tenants are jointly liable for the payment of all rents and all liabilities falling upon the tenants during the tenancy, as well as any breach of the Agreement.

Individually each tenant is responsible for payment of all rent and all liabilities falling upon the tenant, as well as any breach of the Agreement until all payments have been made in full.

What happens if either party wants to end the tenancy early?

There are only limited ways in which this can happen; the landlord cannot make the tenants move out, nor can the tenants lawfully walk away from their obligations to fulfil the contract.

Either party might request of the other that a formal “surrender” of the tenancy be allowed. It would then be up to the parties to agree the terms and conditions of such a surrender. This might include some financial compensation for inconvenience or costs incurred.

Are tenants entitled to have my name & address?

It is a legally binding on us to provide the tenant with the landlord details within 21 days of a formal written request.

What happens if the property is damaged?

Any damages to the property will be paid for from the deposit of the tenant on their departure. We will also look to them for any further payment needed to make up the deficit if required.
